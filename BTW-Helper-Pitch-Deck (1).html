Problem 02

Every quarter, filing BTW is small, frequent, and quietly terrifying.

1.8M Dutch zzp'ers must report their VAT four times a year on a government portal that speaks in coded boxes — reverse charge, self-assessed tax, EU rules — that nobody taught them. Get a box wrong and you overpay or invite a correction. So they pick one of three bad options:

Pay an accountant

€600–€2,500/year for 30 minutes of quarterly work. Overkill for one person with 20 invoices.

Bookkeeping software

MoneyBird & co are full accounting suites. They ask you to become a bookkeeper to use them.

Wing it in Excel

Manual VAT math at 23:00 on deadline day. Free, error-prone, stressful every quarter.

Solution 03

Drop your invoices in.
We hand back a filed return.

BTW Helper reads each invoice with AI, assigns the correct rubriek, handles reverse-charge and currency conversion, and pre-fills a simulator that mirrors the official Belastingdienst form. You review, copy across, and file. Done.

One job, done end to end

Not a ledger, not line items — invoices in, a correct return out. The thing freelancers actually need, and nothing they don't.

You stay in control

We pre-fill; you submit yourself. No risky auto-filing to a tax authority, and every classification is explained in plain language.

Positioning: we sell relief, not bookkeeping. The enemy is deadline-night dread — not a competitor.
